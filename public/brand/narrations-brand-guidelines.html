01 · The story

Every narrative
has two halves.

The Narrations N is built from two mirrored L-shapes that lean toward each other and lock. They are the two halves of every story we help our clients tell — the brand and the audience, the setup and the payoff, the question and the answer.

Between them runs a single diagonal seam in brand green. That seam is the narrative spine — the through-line that pulls the two halves into one figure and turns shapes into meaning. The L-shapes are the green; the seam holds the figure together and the ink half-arms break out at each elbow — that's where the story resolves.

The wordmark drops its own opening letter. The mark is the N. The brand begins by telling, not by labelling — the first letter is already a story being read.

Step 01 Two halves

Two mirrored L-shapes face each other on a 120 × 120 grid. Two voices, not yet in conversation.

Step 02 They lock

The Ls take shape and interlock. The negative space between them is where the story will live.

Step 03 The spine

A single diagonal seam in brand green runs corner to corner — the narrative through-line that ties the two halves into one read.

Step 04 The narrative N

The L-shapes settle into brand green; the seam kisses each elbow and the ink half-arms break out into the corners — the story resolves. The mark reads as N: Narrations.

03 · Logo space

Logo space

For the logo to stay visible, keep clear space equal to the height of the mark around the full lockup. Treat that distance as a hard floor — never allow text, imagery, or another logo inside the boundary.

08 · Usage

Do this. Not that.

The logo is a system, not an asset. These rules keep the mark legible across surfaces and contexts.

Do

  • Use the full lockup the first time the brand appears in any new context.
  • Use the mark alone where the brand name is already present.
  • Keep clear space equal to the height of the mark.
  • Render on plain backgrounds where the green seam stays legible.
  • Use the 1-color variants for embroidery, foil, or single-plate print.

Don't

  • Recolor the mark in any hue other than ink, white, or brand green.
  • Add a stroke, gradient, shadow, or rotation to the lockup.
  • Replace the wordmark text with anything other than "arrations".
  • Place the logo on busy photography that swallows the seam.
  • Stretch the lockup or change its 4.5 : 1 aspect ratio.
